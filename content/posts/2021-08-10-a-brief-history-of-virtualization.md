The concept of virtualization originated in the late 1960s and early 1970s, when IBM starting working on a Time sharing solution.

_Time-sharing_ is a concept that works on the shared usage of computer resources among _n_ number of users. The time-sharing concepts helps in the improving the efficiency.

The time-sharing model was a key milestone in the computer technology. The capacity in a single server is so huge that it is almost impossible for most of the workloads to effectively use it.

The best way to improve the efficiency is to use virtualization.

Virtualization is also very handy in providing abstraction from the physical hardware and thus creating a large aggregated pools of logical resources consisting of Processors, memory, disks, file storage, applications, networking, and offer those resources to users or customers in the form of scalable, consolidated virtual machines.

This is very virtualization became quiet popular in the 1990s. Virtualization helped the companies as they could partition their servers and run legacy apps on multiple operating system types and versions. Servers started becoming more efficient, thereby reducing the costs associated with purchase, set up, cooling, and maintenance. It also helped reduce the vendor lock-in and made the foundation of the cloud computing.
