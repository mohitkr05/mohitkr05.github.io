<div class="column large-7 medium-7">
  <p>
    O-RAN is an alliance developed with an aim to build the specifications for an Open Radio Access Network (O-RAN) ecosystem.
  </p>
  
  <p>
    Open RAN is a concept based on interoperability and standardization of RAN elements.<img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="wp-image-2530 alignright" src="https://dailytechlearning.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/ORANlogo-1024x439.png" alt="" width="459" height="197" srcset="https://www.mohitkr.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/ORANlogo-1024x439.png 1024w, https://www.mohitkr.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/ORANlogo-300x129.png 300w, https://www.mohitkr.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/ORANlogo-768x329.png 768w, https://www.mohitkr.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/ORANlogo.png 1500w" sizes="(max-width: 459px) 100vw, 459px" />
  </p>
  
  <p>
    O-RAN architecture integrates a modular base station software stack on off-the-shelf hardware which allows <em>baseband</em> and radio unit components from discrete suppliers to operate seamlessly together. O-RAN Alliance was founded in February 2018 by AT&T, China Mobile, Deutsche Telekom, NTT DOCOMO and Orange as an German entity in August 2018.
  </p>
</div>

> <p class="">
>   O-RAN Alliance’s mission is to re-shape the RAN industry towards more intelligent, open, virtualized and fully interoperable mobile networks.
> </p>

<p class="">
  The O-RAN alliance is active in the 3 main streams
</p>

  1. O-RAN specification effort which works in extending RAN standards towards openness and intelligence based mechanisms
  2. O-RAN Software Community , which works in development of open software for the RAN (in cooperation with the Linux Foundation)
  3. Testing and integration effort to support O-RAN member companies in testing and integration of their O-RAN implementations

## O-RAN Membership Levels

<p class="">
  O-RAN recognizes two membership categories:
</p>

  * <p class="">
      Member &#8211; a Mobile Network Operator
    </p>

  * <p class="">
      Contributor or Academic Contributor:
    </p>
    
      * Contributor &#8211; includes without limitation: a manufacturer, a vendor, research institute, service provider, network operator not falling into the definition of Mobile Network Operator
      * Academic Contributor &#8211; academic institution, non-industry affiliated research institute or, at the O-RAN Board’s discretion, other non-profit making organization
